# Project Vision

## One sentence

Collective Library is a trust-based knowledge network for local communities — infrastructure that makes invisible trust and knowledge visible, starting with personal books and growing into events, manifests, and local places.

## How the surfaces connect

The product runs on one loop:

> Profile → Signal → Discovery → Contact → Trust → Activity → More Signals

For the full map of every surface (Profile, Books, Wanted, Events, RSVP, Manifest, Spots, Activity, Members, Map, Discord, X, Mastermind, Feedback) and how they feed each other, see [`BUSINESS_PROCESS.md`](./BUSINESS_PROCESS.md). For the voice and visual system, see [`BRAND_AND_VOICE.md`](./BRAND_AND_VOICE.md).

## Core beliefs

- Knowledge does not need a central building.
- Books in homes are still part of a living public collection.
- Trust can be designed through identity and contribution.
- Community participation is a feature, not a side effect.
- The moat is local trust density, not features.

## What we are building

1. **Product**: a web platform that turns personal books, interests, events, manifests, and local places into visible trust signals.
2. **Community**: a social layer for relationships and collaboration — connected through, not replacing, WhatsApp / Instagram / Discord.
3. **Open source**: a contributor ecosystem where people shape direction.

## What we are not building

A list of explicit non-goals lives in [`BUSINESS_PROCESS.md`](./BUSINESS_PROCESS.md) §4. The short version: no internal chat, no payments, no automated X posting, no recommendation engine, no marketplace bloat.

## What success looks like

- More books discoverable across personal libraries.
- More cross-member collaboration and local events.
- Activity per active member that compounds week over week.
- Clearer contributor identity and recognition.
- A replicable model for communities in multiple cities (Phase 5).
